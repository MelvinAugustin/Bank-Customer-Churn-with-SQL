# Bank Churn Prediction Dashboard
![Dashboard](Screenshot 2026-05-08 152540.png)
An interactive machine learning and analytics dashboard built with Python, Streamlit, Scikit-learn, and Plotly for predicting customer churn and exploring customer behavior through advanced visual analytics.

---

# What is Churn?
Customer churn refers to the act of customers leaving a business or discontinuing their relationship with an organization. This can occur for many reasons, including poor customer service, better competitor offers, pricing issues, or lack of engagement. Churn is commonly tracked in industries such as banking, insurance, telecommunications, and subscription-based services. Being able to predict customer churn or identify customers who have a high probability of leaving can significantly improve customer retention strategies.

# Overview

The Bank Churn Prediction Dashboard is designed to help analyze customer retention patterns and predict which customers are likely to leave a bank or financial institution.

# Key Features

## Machine Learning Model Comparison

Compare multiple classification approaches including:
- NORMAL dataset training
- ROS (Random Oversampling)
- RUS (Random Undersampling)

Metrics include:
- Accuracy
- Recall
- F1 Score
- ROC AUC
- Average Probability

Interactive visualizations help evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of each model.

---

## Interactive Analytics Dashboard

Built with Streamlit and Plotly for real-time exploration of data.

Features include:
- Radar charts
- Correlation heatmaps
- Feature importance analysis
- Customer segmentation visuals
- ROC curves
- Confusion matrices
- Distribution analysis
- Customer churn exploration tools

---

## Dynamic Threshold Adjustment

Users can modify the churn probability threshold to explore how prediction sensitivity changes.

This simulates real-world business decision tuning where organizations balance:
- False positives
- False negatives
- Customer retention costs

---

## Feature Importance Analysis

Understand which variables contribute most to churn predictions.

Examples include:
- Age
- Balance

- Credit score
- Product count

This improves interpretability and business understanding of the ML model.
